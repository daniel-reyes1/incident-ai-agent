STAGE 2 — HYPOTHESES (ranked by likelihood)

1.  **Hypothesis: Scope undersized for current device count.**
    *   **Likelihood:** High
    *   **Reasoning:** The incident report explicitly states the problem is "getting worse as more people arrive and connect," which strongly suggests that the demand for IP addresses is exceeding the available supply in the DHCP scope. Even with "no recent known changes," organic growth in user count, BYOD devices, or IoT devices over time can gradually push a previously adequate scope past its capacity.

2.  **Hypothesis: Lease time too long.**
    *   **Likelihood:** High
    *   **Reasoning:** If IP addresses are assigned for an excessively long duration (e.g., several days), they are not released back into the pool quickly enough. As users connect and disconnect throughout the day, the pool becomes depleted more rapidly than addresses can be recycled, leading to the observed "getting worse" pattern, even if the total concurrent device count isn't extraordinarily high at any single moment.

3.  **Hypothesis: Stale leases.**
    *   **Likelihood:** Medium-High
    *   **Reasoning:** A large number of IP addresses could be tied up by devices that are no longer active on the network but still hold leases. This effectively reduces the usable size of the DHCP scope. This often occurs in conjunction with long lease times or after device refreshes where old devices are decommissioned but their leases remain active. This would contribute to the observed exhaustion as available IPs dwindle.

4.  **Hypothesis: Rogue DHCP client or misconfigured device exhausting scope.**
    *   **Likelihood:** Medium
    *   **Reasoning:** While "no recent known changes" makes a *new* rogue device less likely, an existing misconfigured device or a software issue (e.g., a looping application constantly requesting new leases) could suddenly start consuming addresses rapidly, leading to scope exhaustion. The gradual onset as more users connect is less typical for a single rogue *rapidly* exhausting the scope but could still contribute if it's continuously active, compounding general capacity issues.
