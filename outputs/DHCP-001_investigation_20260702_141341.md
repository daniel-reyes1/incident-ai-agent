    *   **Likelihood:** Medium-High

3.  **Hypothesis: Lease Time Too Long for Network Segment Characteristics**
    *   **Likelihood:** Medium-High
    *   **Reasoning:** If the DHCP lease time is excessively long for a segment that experiences high device turnover (e.g., people connecting and disconnecting frequently throughout the day), addresses are not returned to the pool quickly enough. This can exacerbate an already constrained scope or, over time, lead to exhaustion even in a reasonably sized scope by preventing timely recycling of IPs.

4.  **Hypothesis: Rogue DHCP Client or Misconfigured Device Exhausting Scope**
